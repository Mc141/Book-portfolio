Prologue – My Why

Some journeys don’t begin with a clear destination. Mine began with curiosity—about how things work, why systems fail, and how technology can bring clarity to chaos. I never saw software as just code; I saw it as a form of communication—between people and machines, between thought and function.

Beginnings

I grew up in Pretoria, South Africa, where the seed of engineering was planted early. From disassembling electronics to experimenting with Arduino boards, my world slowly shifted from playful curiosity to deliberate design.

At HTS John Vorster, I leaned into math and sciences, eventually graduating as valedictorian. I didn’t chase grades—I chased understanding. But the recognition reassured me that I was on the right path.

The Student Chapter

At Belgium Campus, I found the playground I was looking for. The curriculum wasn’t just theoretical; it was gritty, practical, and rigorous. I dove into C#, Python, and SQL—and found joy in structure and logic. I experimented with both frontend interfaces and backend systems. Each module taught me more about how systems interconnect—both technically and socially.

By second year, I’d become a tutor for web development, programming, and databases. Teaching made me better; explaining concepts sharpened my own understanding. Alongside that, I maintained top academic performance and embraced the idea that you become what you practice.

Skills & Tools

  • Databases: SQL Server, MongoDB, Microsoft Access
  • Current Focus: Learning React, deepening full-stack skills

Projects (Chapters)

Each project I've taken on has taught me something different—about users, about architecture, and often, about myself.

  • Chapter 1: A budgeting app for students built with ASP.NET MVC and SQL Server
  • Chapter 2: An Arduino-powered soil moisture system for chicken farms
  • Chapter 3: A personal portfolio site (this one!) made as a turnable book using JavaScript

Teaching & Tutoring

In 2023, I worked as a science tutor at Meta Centre. I helped nine students—not just with equations, but with confidence. In 2024, I became a university tutor for web development and databases at Belgium Campus. Teaching has become part of my learning loop.
