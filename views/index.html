Prologue – My Why

Some journeys begin with a road map. Mine began with a question: How does the digital world really work? I didn’t want to just use software—I wanted to understand its heartbeat, its mechanics, its logic. What began as childlike wonder slowly grew into a mission: to build, to solve, and to contribute.

This story is not a linear checklist of skills—it’s a layered process of growth, shaped by late nights of coding, moments of clarity during tutoring sessions, and a growing sense of calling. Technology, to me, is a tool. Purpose is the goal.

Beginnings

I grew up in Pretoria, South Africa, where wires, remote controls, and motherboards became my playground. I was that kid—the one taking apart gadgets not to destroy, but to decode their secrets. That hands-on curiosity led me to HTS John Vorster, where maths and physical sciences became my foundation stones.

I didn’t just study—I thrived. Graduating as valedictorian with six distinctions wasn’t just a title, but a testimony to countless hours of discovery, problem-solving, and persistence. I wasn’t chasing marks—I was chasing meaning. And in those early years, I found it through logic, patterns, and the magic of engineering principles.

The Student Chapter

At Belgium Campus, I stepped into an entirely new arena—one that demanded not only theoretical sharpness, but practical creativity. With every module, I moved from curiosity to competence. C#, Python, JavaScript—they became tools in my toolkit. And more recently, Java, JSP, Servlets, and even Swing broadened the horizon.

By my second year, I didn’t just consume knowledge—I shared it. I began tutoring fellow students in web development, databases, and software principles. Helping others helped me, too. It clarified my thinking, deepened my empathy, and reinforced one truth: that education is a cycle of giving and growing.

Now, as a third-year student preparing for my final stretch, I am looking ahead—with intention. I’m seeking an internship opportunity that will allow me to apply what I’ve learned, challenge what I think I know, and contribute meaningfully to a real-world team. I’m not just looking to work—I’m looking to serve, grow, and leave a mark.

Projects (Chapters)

Every project I’ve completed tells a story—of trial, iteration, and insight:

  • Chapter 1: A budgeting app built with ASP.NET MVC and SQL Server, designed to help students manage their finances practically and intuitively.
  • Chapter 2: An Arduino-based soil moisture system, tailored for chicken farms—where hardware met software in solving a real-world agricultural challenge.
  • Chapter 3: This very portfolio—a digital book styled with JavaScript and Turn.js—representing my design sensibilities and technical voice.

Teaching & Tutoring

In 2023, I started tutoring high school learners in Mathematics and Physical Sciences at Meta Centre. What began as an opportunity to support others quickly became a passion for teaching and mentoring.

In 2024, I transitioned into tutoring at Belgium Campus, helping peers in areas like programming, databases, and web development. Tutoring grounded me in the principle that real learning happens when we teach, and that knowledge shared is knowledge deepened.
